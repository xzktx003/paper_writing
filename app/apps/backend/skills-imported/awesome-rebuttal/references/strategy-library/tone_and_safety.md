# Tone and Safety Rules

## Preferred tone

- appreciative but not performative
- concise and evidence-first
- confident but bounded
- reviewer-facing and AC-legible

## Avoid

- “The reviewer is wrong”
- “Obviously” / “clearly” when correcting misunderstandings
- unsupported “we have shown” claims
- promises to add experiments/results that do not exist
- manipulative flattery toward AC/reviewers
- anonymity-breaking details

## Safer patterns

- “We apologize for the lack of clarity; the paper currently states ...”
- “To address this, we can add the following clarification ...”
- “This is a valid limitation; we will revise the claim to ...”
- “Within the rebuttal window, we can provide ...; a full extension would require ...”
