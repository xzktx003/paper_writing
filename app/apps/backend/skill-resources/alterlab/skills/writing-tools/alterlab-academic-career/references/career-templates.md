## Research Statement Templates

### Template A: Three-Thread Research Program

```markdown
# Research Statement: [Your Name]

My research investigates [BIG QUESTION] at the intersection of [Field A]
and [Field B]. Through [APPROACH], I have [KEY ACCOMPLISHMENT]. My work
has resulted in [X] publications in journals including [Top Journal 1]
and [Top Journal 2], [Y] in total external funding, and [Z] trained
graduate students. Below I describe the three interconnected threads of
my research program and my plans for the next five years.

## Thread 1: [Descriptive Title]

[WHY this matters — 2-3 sentences on the problem]

[WHAT you did — key methods and innovations]

[WHAT you found — 2-3 key findings with citations to your work]

[SO WHAT — significance for the field]

## Thread 2: [Descriptive Title]

[Same structure as Thread 1]

[Show how this connects to Thread 1]

## Thread 3: [Descriptive Title]

[Same structure]

[Show how all three threads form a coherent program]

## Current Projects

[What you are working on now, with expected outcomes]

[Funding status: funded, under review, planned]

## Future Directions (2026-2031)

[Specific Project A]
- Research question: ...
- Method: ...
- Expected outcomes: ...
- Funding target: [NSF Program, NIH Institute, etc.]

[Specific Project B]
- [Same structure]

## Fit with [Department Name]

[How your work complements existing strengths]
[Specific faculty you would collaborate with — be genuine]
[Facilities or resources at the institution that would advance your work]
[Students you would train and the skills they would develop]
```

### Template B: Narrative Arc Research Statement

```markdown
# Research Statement: [Your Name]

[OPENING VIGNETTE — a brief, compelling example that illustrates why your
research matters. This might be a real-world problem, a surprising finding,
or a gap in understanding that motivates your work.]

Since beginning my scholarly career in [Year], I have developed a research
program that [APPROACH TO THE PROBLEM], contributing [X] publications,

[Deeper explanation of the gap in knowledge or the real-world challenge]

## Key Contributions
```

## Teaching Philosophy Templates

### Template: Principles-Based

```markdown
# Teaching Philosophy: [Your Name]

## My Core Belief

[One powerful sentence that captures your teaching philosophy]

I arrived at this conviction through [brief origin — experience, training,
or observation that shaped your approach].

## Principle 1: [e.g., "Learning Requires Active Construction"]

I believe [PRINCIPLE]. Research in [learning science area] shows that
[EVIDENCE FOR THE PRINCIPLE].

In practice, this means [WHAT YOU DO IN THE CLASSROOM]. For example,
in my [Course Name] course, I [SPECIFIC ACTIVITY OR APPROACH].
The result was [EVIDENCE OF IMPACT — evaluations, grades, student feedback].

## Principle 2: [e.g., "Assessment Should Drive Learning, Not Just Measure It"]

[Same structure: principle → evidence → practice → specific example → outcome]

## Principle 3: [e.g., "Inclusive Classrooms Require Intentional Design"]

[Same structure]

## Evidence of Effectiveness

[Quantitative: evaluation scores, grade distributions, retention rates]
[Qualitative: student quotes, peer observations, teaching awards]

## Growth and Future Goals

My teaching continues to evolve. Currently, I am [LEARNING/TRYING].
At [Institution], I would be excited to [SPECIFIC PLANS — new courses,
pedagogical innovations, collaborations with teaching center].
```

## Cover Letter Template

```markdown
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Search Committee Chair Name, if known]
[Department Name]
[Institution Name]
[Address]

Dear [Name / Members of the Search Committee],

I am writing to apply for the position of [EXACT TITLE] in the Department
of [NAME] at [INSTITUTION], as advertised in [WHERE YOU SAW IT]. I am
currently [YOUR CURRENT POSITION] at [YOUR INSTITUTION], where I
[ONE SENTENCE ABOUT YOUR WORK]. My research on [TOPIC], combined with my
commitment to [TEACHING/MENTORING/SERVICE], make me a strong fit for this
position and your department's strengths in [AREA].

[RESEARCH PARAGRAPH — 4-6 sentences]
My research program investigates [TOPIC]. I have published [X] articles in
journals including [TOP JOURNALS] and secured [FUNDING AMOUNT] in external
funding from [AGENCIES]. My current project, [BRIEF DESCRIPTION], addresses
[SIGNIFICANCE]. This work aligns with your department's expertise in [AREA],
and I see exciting opportunities for collaboration with [FACULTY NAMES]
on [SPECIFIC TOPICS].

[TEACHING PARAGRAPH — 4-6 sentences]
I am an experienced teacher of [COURSES], having taught [NUMBER] courses at
[INSTITUTIONS]. My approach centers on [BRIEF PHILOSOPHY]. I am prepared to
teach [COURSES FROM THE AD] and would be eager to develop a new course in
[AREA] that would complement your current offerings. My student evaluations
consistently [EVIDENCE].

[FIT PARAGRAPH — 3-4 sentences]
Beyond research and teaching, I would contribute to [INSTITUTION] through
[SERVICE, DEI, OTHER CONTRIBUTIONS]. I am drawn to [INSTITUTION] because
[GENUINE, SPECIFIC REASONS — not just "it's a great school"].

I have enclosed my [LIST OF MATERIALS]. I would welcome the opportunity to
discuss how my work aligns with your department's goals. Thank you for your
consideration.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Elevator Pitch Templates

### 30-Second Version (Networking)

```
"I'm [Name], a [position] at [institution]. I study [TOPIC] using [METHOD].
Recently, I found that [KEY FINDING], which matters because [SIGNIFICANCE].
I'd love to hear about your work on [something specific to them]."
```

### 60-Second Version (Job Talk Introduction)

```
"Thank you for the invitation. I'm [Name], and my research asks [BIG QUESTION].
This matters because [REAL-WORLD SIGNIFICANCE]. Over the past [X] years,
I've approached this through [METHOD/FRAMEWORK], which led to [2-3 KEY FINDINGS].
Today I'll focus on [SPECIFIC PROJECT], which reveals [MAIN ARGUMENT].
I'll also share how this work opens new directions in [FUTURE AREA] that
connect to the exciting work happening here in [DEPARTMENT STRENGTH]."
```

## Timeline and Checklist Resources

### Academic Job Market Timeline (Humanities/Social Sciences)

```
April-June (Year Before):
  □ Draft research statement
  □ Draft teaching philosophy
  □ Update CV
  □ Identify letter writers and ask them
  □ Research target institutions and positions

July-August:
  □ Revise all documents based on feedback
  □ Prepare writing samples
  □ Draft diversity statement
  □ Build/update website
  □ Attend job market workshops

September-November:
  □ Applications are due (peak season)
  □ Tailor cover letters for each position
  □ Submit materials via Interfolio, Academic Jobs Online, or institutional portals
  □ Continue applying as new positions are posted

December-February:
  □ First-round interviews (conference, Zoom, or phone)
  □ Prepare for common interview questions
  □ Practice job talk

January-March:
  □ Campus visits (fly-outs)
  □ Job talks, teaching demonstrations
  □ Meetings with faculty, students, deans

March-May:
  □ Offers and negotiations
  □ Accept position
  □ Notify other institutions and letter writers
```

### Tenure Preparation Timeline

```
Year 1-2:
  □ Understand tenure criteria (get them in writing)
  □ Identify mentor(s) inside and outside department
  □ Begin documentation system (teaching, research, service)
  □ Submit 1-2 papers from dissertation/postdoc
  □ Apply for internal and external grants

Year 3 (often mid-tenure review):
  □ Prepare mid-tenure dossier
  □ Get feedback from review committee
  □ Adjust research and teaching plans based on feedback
  □ Draft preliminary tenure narrative

Year 4-5:
  □ Build record toward external funding (if expected)
  □ Mentor graduate students toward completion
  □ Develop new courses or programs
  □ Increase national visibility (invited talks, leadership)

Year 6 (tenure year):
  □ Compile complete dossier
  □ Write tenure narrative (personal statement)
  □ Suggest and vet external reviewers
  □ Submit dossier (typically September-October)
  □ Wait (decisions typically spring of Year 7)
```
