# Experiment Report — Learning the Burgers Solution Operator with an FNO

> **Provenance: MEASURED.** Every number below comes from `results.json`
> (`provenance.mode = "measured"`), produced by `experiment/run_all.py` on CPU.
> Ground truth is generated by a pseudo-spectral solver; no dataset was downloaded.
> Generated with the `ai4s-skills` experiment-suite skill — **human expert review
> recommended** before any downstream use.

## 1. Problem statement
Can a Fourier Neural Operator (FNO) learn the solution operator
`G: u(·,0) → u(·,1)` of the 1D viscous Burgers' equation, and does it stay accurate
at grid resolutions it never saw during training? We compare against a
fully-connected MLP and a 1-D CNN.

## 2. Design rationale
Burgers at viscosity `ν = 1e-3` forms steep, shock-like fronts before mild
diffusion, so the operator is strongly nonlinear — a meaningful test. The three
architectures are chosen to isolate one factor each: the MLP has no structural bias
and is grid-locked; the CNN is local and translation-equivariant; the FNO adds
*global* spectral mixing and is discretisation-invariant by construction. Any
accuracy or transfer gap therefore points to the value of global spectral kernels.
Full rationale: `experiment_design.md`. Data binding: `data_contract.md`.

## 3. Method
Ground truth: pseudo-spectral integrating-factor RK4 solver (2/3-rule dealiasing),
2000 time steps to `T = 1`. Initial conditions from a Gaussian random field. FNO:
16 modes, width 32, 4 Fourier layers, with an `(u₀, x)` lifting. All models
minimise mean relative-L2 error with Adam (lr 1e-3, cosine decay, weight decay
1e-4), 50 epochs, batch 20. Code: `experiment/{data,model,train,run_all}.py`.

## 4. Setup
1000 train / 200 val / 200 test functions at grid 128; test sets regenerated at
256/512/1024 for the super-resolution probe. Main comparison over 3 seeds
(resampled training data); ablations single-seed. Hardware: laptop CPU, PyTorch
2.5.1. Total wall-clock: ~1235 s (~20 min).

## 5. Results

### 5.1 Accuracy (3 seeds, mean ± std) — `fig2_comparison`, `fig1_qualitative`
| Model | Test rel-L2 (%) | Params |
|---|---|---|
| **FNO** | **6.67 ± 0.07** | 74,209 |
| MLP | 22.47 ± 0.59 | 657,024 |
| CNN | 68.12 ± 0.16 | 41,793 |

The FNO is **3.4× more accurate than the MLP and 10.2× more accurate than the CNN**,
with ~9× fewer parameters than the MLP. Qualitatively (`fig1_qualitative`), the FNO
reproduces the shock front; both baselines concentrate their error there.

### 5.2 Discretisation invariance (zero-shot super-resolution) — `fig3_superres`
| Eval grid | 128 | 256 | 512 | 1024 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FNO rel-L2 (%) | 6.74 | 7.85 | 8.09 | 7.18 |
| CNN rel-L2 (%) | 67.9 | 74.4 | 74.0 | 72.7 |

An FNO trained only at grid 128 stays in a narrow 6.7–8.1% band with **no blow-up**
as the grid is refined 8×. The CNN stays an order of magnitude worse; the MLP cannot
be evaluated at all (grid-locked first layer).

### 5.3 Ablations (single seed — trends) — `fig4_ablation`
- **Fourier modes** 4 → 16: 8.10% → 6.84% → 6.69% → 6.74% (improves, then saturates
  once modes cover the active band).
- **Training size** 200/500/1000: 12.01% → 8.45% → 6.74% (monotone improvement).

## 6. Analysis
Both FNO claims hold on this benchmark. The accuracy gap over the CNN isolates the
value of *global* spectral mixing (both share weights spatially, but only the FNO is
non-local). The near-flat super-resolution curve is the practical headline: train at
a convenient resolution, deploy at another with no retraining. The modes ablation
matches the spectral-truncation view — capacity saturates once the retained band
spans the solution's active frequencies.

## 7. Limitations
- One equation, one spatial dimension, fixed GRF input prior; conclusions are
  conditional on that prior.
- Ablations are single-seed (trend, not significance).
- Baselines are untuned reference points, not upper bounds.
- Teaching-scale budget (small models, 50 epochs, CPU): absolute errors are not a
  maximal-accuracy FNO. A production setup would use more data, more modes, and GPU.
- Super-resolution reuses the seed-0 models; the MLP is excluded by construction.
